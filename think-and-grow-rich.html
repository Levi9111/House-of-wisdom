Think and Grow Rich

Napoleon Hill – The Complete Philosophy of Achievement
This is not just a book—it is a philosophy of personal achievement, distilled from over twenty years of study into the world's most successful individuals[citation:5]. Hill believed that financial riches were just one manifestation of a success that begins in the mind. The principles below are designed to be studied and applied, not merely read[citation:2]. As Hill wrote, "All achievement, all earned riches, have their beginning in an idea"[citation:1].
Principle 1
Desire: The Starting Point of All Achievement
A burning, pulsating desire for a definite goal is the non-negotiable fuel for the journey to success. It is more than a wish—it is an obsession[citation:1].
The Core Lesson
Every great fortune began with a single, clearly defined desire that was held with intense conviction. Wishing will not bring riches, but a burning desire that is backed by a definite plan and persistence will[citation:1].
"If you do not see great riches in your imagination, you will never see them in your bank balance." – Napoleon Hill[citation:1]
The 6-Step Formula to Turn Desire into Gold[citation:1][citation:2]
  1. Fix the exact amount of money you desire.
  2. Determine exactly what you will give in return for this money.
  3. Establish a definite date when you intend to possess this money.
  4. Create a definite plan and begin at once to carry it out.
  5. Write a clear, concise statement of the amount, time limit, what you'll give, and your plan.
  6. Read your statement aloud twice daily—upon waking and before sleeping—and see, feel, and believe yourself already in possession of the money.
Practical Exercise: The Written Definite Chief Aim
Complete the 6-step formula above. Write it by hand. Place it where you will see it every day. This document is the crystallized form of your desire.
Principle 2
Faith: The Visualization & Belief in Attainment
Faith is the "head chemist of the mind." When faith is blended with thought, the subconscious mind instantly picks up the vibration and translates it into its spiritual equivalent[citation:1].
The Core Lesson
Faith is a state of mind that can be cultivated through repetition of positive affirmations to the subconscious mind (autosuggestion). It is the only known antidote for failure[citation:1]. Faith removes limitations and allows you to tap into what Hill called Infinite Intelligence[citation:2].
The Self-Confidence Formula[citation:1]
To build faith, repeat this formula daily:
  • I know I have the ability to achieve my definite purpose, therefore I demand persistent action toward its attainment.
  • I realize my dominating thoughts will reproduce themselves physically, so I will concentrate for 30 minutes daily on thinking of the person I intend to become.
  • I know through autosuggestion, any desire I persistently hold will find expression, so I will devote 10 minutes daily to demanding self-confidence.
  • I have written my definite chief aim and will never stop until I attain sufficient self-confidence for it.
  • I will engage in no transaction that does not benefit all whom it affects.
Practical Exercise: Affirmation & Visualization
Twice daily, find a quiet place. Close your eyes and repeat your Definite Chief Aim with emotion. Vividly visualize yourself already in possession of your goal—see the scenes, hear the sounds, feel the emotions[citation:2].
Principle 3
Autosuggestion: The Medium for Influencing the Subconscious
Autosuggestion is self-suggestion. It is the means of communication between the conscious, thinking mind and the subconscious, creative mind[citation:1].
The Core Lesson
Any thought, plan, or purpose you repeatedly hold in your conscious mind, mixed with strong emotion (faith), will eventually be accepted by your subconscious mind, which then works tirelessly to translate it into physical reality[citation:1]. This is the practical application of the Law of Attraction[citation:2].
"Your belief or faith is the element that determines the action of your subconscious mind." – Napoleon Hill[citation:1]
How to Apply Autosuggestion
  • Be Definite: Use your written statement from the Desire principle.
  • Be Emotional: Read it with feeling, conviction, and faith.
  • Be Repetitive: The subconscious accepts instructions through repetition. Perform the ritual every morning and every night without fail[citation:1].
Practical Exercise: The Nightly & Morning Ritual
Make your autosuggestion session non-negotiable. Before sleep and upon waking, when your mind is most impressionable, read your Definite Chief Aim aloud. Feel the truth of it. This plants the seed of desire deep into your subconscious soil.
Principle 4
Specialized Knowledge
General knowledge has little power to attract riches. It is organized, intelligently directed, specialized knowledge—applied to a definite plan—that leads to wealth[citation:1][citation:2].
Principle 5
Imagination
The workshop of the mind where all plans are created. Everything humanity has created or achieved began as a thought, shaped in the faculty of imagination[citation:2].
Principle 6
Organized Planning
A burning desire must crystallize into action. This is achieved through a practical, organized plan, often created with the aid of a Mastermind group[citation:1][citation:2].
Principle 7
Decision
The mastery of procrastination. Successful people make decisions promptly and change them slowly, if at all. Those who fail decide slowly and change quickly[citation:1][citation:2].
Principle 8
Persistence
The sustained effort necessary to induce faith. Will-power is the conscious act; persistence is the unconscious habit. It is the only quality through which failure is converted into achievement[citation:1][citation:2].
Principle 9
Power of the Master Mind
The coordination of knowledge and effort between two or more people who work toward a definite purpose in a spirit of harmony. It is the "driving force" behind great achievements[citation:2].
Principle 10
The Mystery of Sex Transmutation
The switching of the mind from thoughts of physical expression to thoughts of some other nature. Sexual energy is a potent creative force that can be redirected into channels of creative work and higher achievement[citation:2][citation:6].
Principle 11
The Subconscious Mind
The connecting link between the finite mind of man and Infinite Intelligence. It is the "chemical laboratory" where thoughts are mixed with emotion and prepared for translation into reality[citation:1][citation:6].
Principle 12
The Brain
A broadcasting and receiving station for thought. Every human brain is capable of picking up vibrations of thought released by other brains, which explains the phenomenon of "hunches" and ideas that seem to come from nowhere[citation:6].
Principle 13
The Sixth Sense
The door to the temple of wisdom. It is that part of the subconscious mind which has been referred to as creative imagination, intuition, and "hunches." It becomes reliable only after mastery of the other twelve principles[citation:6].
The Final Step
Outwitting the Six Ghosts of Fear
Before your mind can become success-conscious, it must be cleared of the six basic fears: Poverty, Criticism, Ill Health, Loss of Love, Old Age, and Death. These fears paralyze faith and initiative[citation:4][citation:6].
A comprehensive reference to Napoleon Hill's Philosophy of Achievement. The true riches Hill defines include a positive mental attitude, sound health, harmony in relationships, freedom from fear, and financial security[citation:1].
